Security & Reliability

We keep your website stable and recoverable using straightforward controls: secure access, routine updates, backups, and monitoring. The goal is to reduce common failures and make support predictable.

What we do by default

These practices are standard across managed clients.

  • SSL/HTTPS: encrypted traffic and HTTPS enforcement.
  • Backups: automated backups with a defined restore process.
  • Updates: WordPress core/plugin updates under a consistent policy.
  • Access control: least-privilege access; admin access limited to what’s necessary.
  • Monitoring: uptime monitoring and basic health checks.

What we don’t claim

We do not provide compliance certification or guarantee that a website is “hack-proof.” We focus on practical risk reduction and recoverability.

Shared responsibility

We manage the hosting and website platform. You manage business decisions and any third-party accounts you control (domain registrar, email provider, payment processor).

  • Client-owned accounts: use MFA when available and keep recovery info current.
  • Content: you approve content and provide accurate business details.
  • Licenses: third-party subscriptions (email, premium plugins) are billed separately unless stated.

If something goes wrong

  • Outage: we investigate and restore service when the issue is hosting/platform related.
  • Breakage after updates: we roll back or remediate within the supported stack.
  • Restore: we use available backups to recover the site to a known good state.

For complex requests (custom code, unusual plugins, high-risk changes), we scope separately to avoid destabilizing your environment.
